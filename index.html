How to make a Balloon Dog

Want to create balloon art, but don’t know how to start? Making a balloon dog is a great way to create entertainment in your home or on-the-go! This text will teach you how to make a balloon dog in 11 informative steps. It only takes eight minutes. Read carefully and have fun!

Materials Needed:

  • Modeling or long balloon
  • Balloon Pump

STEP 1

Stretch the balloon holding it long ways.

Tip: Gently tug to avoid it breaking.

STEP 2

Take your balloon and bring the part with a hole over the nozzle of the pump. Hold this in place so it’s secure and doesn’t fly away.

STEP 3

Inflate the balloon using a balloon pump. Inflate until there’s roughly two inches of deflated balloon on the opposite side as the hole.

Tip: Follow the instructions on your specific balloon pump as the designs may vary.

STEP 4

Tie a knot on the opposite side as the hole. Do this by stretching the (hole) end of the balloon and wrapping it around two fingers. Give yourself enough space to to thread the closed end through. Pull this secure to tie the knot.

Tip: Tying the knot may take a couple tries. Don’t give up!

STEP 5

Starting on the end with the knot, twist three small bubbles that are equal in size. The bubbles should be roughly the length of your palm.

Tip: The bubbles should take up a bit less than half the size of the balloon length.

STEP 6

Lock twist. Take the inner two bubbles and hold them parallel to each other and twist to secure them.

Tip: After this step, you should start to see a head and snout.

STEP 7

Twist three more bubbles, all the same size, directly behind the head.

STEP 8

Lock twist again, as you did in STEP 6. this should create the legs and neck.

Tip: You should start to see the legs and neck.

STEP 9

Grab behind the legs and twist a slightly longer bubble. This will be it’s torso.

Tip: The torso bubble should be the length of your entire hand.

STEP 10

After the torso bubble, using the remaining part of the inflated balloon, twist three small bubbles of equal size.

Tip: The bubbles should be roughly the length of your palm.

STEP 11

Lock twist, as you did in STEP 6 and STEP 8, but on the closed end of the balloon. This will create the hind legs and tail.

Tip: Twist until the deflated tip of the balloon is facing upwards. This will make a tail shape.

Congratulations, you made yourself an inflatable canine!
